\chapter{The Cultural-Algorithm Program: Searching for the Best Case}
\label{ch:cultural-program}

\paragraph{Normative status.}
Author-directed round: the thesis must include the use of the cultural
algorithm, either tuning the Levenberg--Marquardt continuation that
leads the monolithic remediation program or acting on the
FE\textsuperscript{2} coupling, and the comparison basis for every
campaign is now the \emph{expected physical response of the structural
element} (the Timoshenko fiber column, plateau
$\approx 23.5$\,kN, zigzag $\le 4.5\%$), with the hand-tuned two-way
case D (force clamp $25\%$ + staggered 12; $27.0$\,kN, $23.5\%$) as
the standing mark to beat. The formulation follows the author's
tuned-inerter-damper optimisation paper
\cite{LaraValenciaEchavarriaValencia2024} and the extensions
catalogued in the cultural-algorithm literature
\cite{Reynolds1994Cultural,ReynoldsPeng2004,Maheri2021CulturalSurvey}.

\section{Semantic generalisation of the module}
\label{sec:ca-semantics}

The \path{src/algorithms/cultural} module was reviewed so that no
class commits to the \emph{genetic} instantiation of the framework:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{Individual} carries \emph{traits} and a fitness. The
        solution vector is Reynolds' behavioural-trait vector; calling
        it a genome would bind the framework to one population
        substrate.
  \item The population space is a \texttt{PopulationModel} policy with
        two operators, \texttt{propose} (parent selection and, if the
        model uses it, recombination) and \texttt{vary} (the model's
        own variation). \texttt{EvolutionaryProgrammingModel} (binary
        tournament + Gaussian perturbation, the classical CA pairing)
        is the default and reproduces the historical behaviour
        bit-for-bit; \texttt{GeneticRecombinationModel}
        (two-tournament BLX-$\alpha$ blend) is provided to make the
        point executable: the genetic algorithm is \emph{one}
        instantiation of the population space, not the framework.
  \item The belief space now offers the five knowledge sources of the
        literature: \emph{normative} (per-trait elite intervals),
        \emph{situational} (best exemplar), \emph{domain}
        (fitness-weighted elite consensus), \emph{history} (temporal
        trend of the generation bests over a sliding window) and
        \emph{topographic} (per-trait occupancy histogram, resampling
        inside the most-visited cell). All are duck-typed policies
        checked by the \texttt{KnowledgeSource} concept, and the
        config knob formerly named after mutation is now the model's
        \texttt{variation\_sigma\_frac}.
\end{itemize}

The unit evidence (\path{tests/test_algorithms_cultural_algorithm.cpp})
keeps the original convergence, box-respect, elitism and determinism
checks and adds two: the five-source belief space \emph{improves} the
multimodal Rastrigin benchmark over the two-source configuration
($-0.87$ vs $-3.01$ at equal budget --- the domain and topographic
sources pay for themselves on multimodal landscapes), and the genetic
instantiation converges on the sphere benchmark through the same loop
with the belief space untouched.

\section{Campaign design: tuning the winning solver against the
physical target}
\label{sec:ca-campaigns}

The round-1--4 remediation program (Caps.~\ref{ch:kinematic-regularization}
and prior) left the energy line search as the standing monolithic
remedy and redirected every comparison to the structural-element
response. The CA program turns that redirection into an objective.
The window-replay harness of Phase P3 (checkpoint at step $k_0$,
re-play $m$ steps per candidate, restore) is reused with a
\emph{physics-anchored} fitness (v2, opt-in through
\texttt{KOBATHE\_CA\_VTARGET} / \texttt{KOBATHE\_CA\_WZZ}; defaults
reproduce v1):
\[
  f = \frac{n_{\mathrm{conv}}}{m}
    - \max\!\Bigl(0, \frac{|V|_{\mathrm{peak}}}{V_{\mathrm{Timo}}}-1\Bigr)
    - 0.2\,\bar\imath
    - w_{zz}\,\frac{\#\text{flips}}{\#\text{monotone steps}},
\]
so a candidate is rewarded for converging, for keeping the window's
peak at or below the \emph{physical plateau} $V_{\mathrm{Timo}}$
(rather than below the run's own envelope, which tolerates inherited
spikes), and for a smooth force path inside the window. Two campaigns
bracket the two anomalies the reference line-search run still
exhibits on the $2{\times}2{\times}4$ protocol:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
campaign & window & brackets & budget \\
\midrule
\path{ca2_w112} & $112{:}12$ & $+150$\,mm peak and its reversal &
  pop 10 $\times$ 12 gen \\
\path{ca2_w156} & $156{:}12$ & $+200$\,mm loading anomaly (step
  161) and reversal & pop 10 $\times$ 12 gen \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Both campaigns run with the line search on ($V_{\mathrm{Timo}} =
0.0237$\,MN, $w_{zz}=0.5$, seed 20260715, top fraction $0.25$), and
each closes with a full-protocol validation run under the best traits
(phase C of the tuner script), summarised against the line-search
reference. Per-generation best/mean fitness and the best-traits
trajectory persist to \path{ca_history.csv} --- the convergence curves
that document the metaheuristic at work.

\paragraph{Series ca2 --- a robust optimum, and an objective-design
lesson.} Both campaigns completed their $10\times 12$ budget with the
full-protocol validation run of phase C. The window verdicts differ
--- $112{:}12$ is achievable (best window fitness $+0.33$) while
$156{:}12$ stays unconquered (best $-0.47$, no candidate converges
the anomaly window strictly) --- yet both searches land in the
\emph{same trait family}, which makes the optimum reproducible across
windows:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrr@{}}
\toprule
trait & \path{ca2_w112} & \path{ca2_w156} \\
\midrule
$\log_{10}\mu_0$        & $-3.66$ & $-3.92$ \\
grow / drop             & $5.29$ / $0.56$ & $6.81$ / $0.34$ \\
stagnation cap          & $13$ & $17$ \\
predictor max scale     & $4.16$ & $3.88$ \\
acceptance floor        & $10^{-3}$ & $10^{-3}$ \\
reversal subdiv / steps & $2/1$ & $3/4$ \\
\midrule
full-protocol noconv    & $\mathbf{53}$ & $\mathbf{54}$ \\
full-protocol $|V|_{\max}$ [MN] & $0.061$ & $0.062$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Against the line-search reference (noconv 78, $|V|_{\max}=0.049$) the
tuned vectors cut the regularized states by a third --- tiny initial
$\mu$, aggressive growth, a loose $10^{-3}$ acceptance floor and mild
reversal sub-incrementation let the continuation converge where the
reference stagnates --- and they halve the zigzag of the $200$\,mm
block ($15.9$ vs $34.1\%$ for w156). The price appears exactly where
the objective could not see it. Outside the tuned window the peaks
grow to $0.061$\,MN ($51.3$\,kN in the $150$\,mm block for w156
against $32.5$ of the reference), and the reactivated
sub-incrementation is the mechanism the early campaign had already
flagged: smaller post-reversal steps converge \emph{more firmly} to
whatever branch is nearest, spurious ones included. The lesson is
about objective design, not about the optimizer. A physics-anchored
fitness evaluated on a single window rewards local exploitation at
global expense, so the v3 objective must span all critical windows
(or the capped protocol, which is precisely what the
FE\textsuperscript{2} tuner of \autoref{sec:ca-fe2} already does
through \texttt{--steps-cap}). The per-generation curves of both
campaigns persist in \path{ca_history.csv} and the comparative figure
\path{data/output/ca_convergence.png}.

\section{The FE\textsuperscript{2} coupling as the second search
space}
\label{sec:ca-fe2}

The strongest thesis showcase after the solver instance is the
coupling itself: case D was \emph{hand-tuned} (force-gap clamp
$25\%$, staggered cap 12), and the two-way evidence shows its
quality is controlled by exactly the kind of low-dimensional,
expensive-to-probe hyperparameter vector the CA is built for ---
clamp fraction, staggered cap, relaxation factor, engagement step.
The planned instance reuses the same module over that four-trait
space with a reduced-protocol objective (single $100$\,mm cycle,
coarse RVE) whose fitness combines the distance to the Timoshenko
plateau, the window zigzag and the coupling-convergence rate; the
full-protocol validation then confronts the CA-found configuration
with case D on all four blocks. Implementation seam: the in-process
objective wraps the same \texttt{MultiscaleAnalysis} entry the column
driver uses, so no new physics code is involved.

\paragraph{The coupling instance closes: the CA matches the hand-tuned
case and improves its regularity.} The process-level tuner
(\path{fall_n_ca_fe2_tuner}, full five-source belief space) ran its
$6\times 6$ budget overnight --- 36 evaluations, each a complete driver
run capped at 64 steps --- with the fitness anchored to the physical
plateau. The best fitness climbed from $0.585$ to $0.810$ and the
search converged on a coherent basin: force clamp $0.165$, relaxation
$0.769$, staggered cap $19$, and engagement at step $39$ --- the CA
\emph{rediscovered} the engagement-at-reversal principle on its own
(step 39 is the second in-window reversal), and every high-fitness
candidate shares it. The full-protocol validation settles the caveat
that the capped objective might have simply shortened the coupled
exposure:

\begin{center}

\toprule

(hand)} \\

All 196 macro steps converge (noconv 0, like case D). The peaks sit in
the same class ($+17$--$22\%$ over the $23.1$\,kN plateau), the large
blocks are \emph{smoother} than the hand-tuned mark (200\,mm total
variation $0.09$\,MN --- the Timoshenko trace's own value), and the
residual roughness concentrates in the $100$\,mm block because the
discovered engagement lands mid-cycle there. In 36 evaluations the
metaheuristic reproduced what took a multi-round manual campaign to
find, and shifted the trade-off toward global regularity. The
remaining refinement is engagement phasing (aligning the start step
with a block boundary), which the trait bounds can encode directly in
the next round.
